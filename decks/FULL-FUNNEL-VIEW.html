2 · What big tech already does in personalization

Personalization + auto-optimization is solved — and impressive. We don't pretend otherwise.

Meta — Advantage+
Give it a URL + budget; AI generates the creative, picks the audience, places it, and bids. Auto-generates creative variations and personalizes backgrounds per audience. 65% of advertisers scaling on it (Apr 2026).
Gap: engagement-optimized B2C ads; creative is unverified and needs human review; walled garden.
Google — Performance Max / AI Max
Keyword-free campaigns across all six Google properties; Gemini generates assets; ML bidding captures search intent automatically.
Gap: optimizes for clicks/conversions, not claim accuracy; no source-grounding of generated copy.
Salesforce — Agentforce (Einstein)
Autonomous marketing agents that optimize campaigns, generate content, and adapt journeys in real time; predictive scoring that learns from engagement, conversion, and revenue.
Gap: generated content isn't claim-verified; the agent optimizes outcomes, with truth left to human review.
Adobe — Journey Optimizer + Sensei
Real-time CDP, AI-native journey orchestration, predictive everything, Firefly content generation — the heavy stack for global B2C.
Gap: built for scale and engagement, not regulated claim substantiation.
Netflix / Lyft / Yahoo — contextual bandits
The auto-A/B technique itself: contextual bandits personalize artwork, offers, and recommendations, beating classic A/B by adapting to each user in real time. Mature, well-understood.
Gap: optimizes engagement only — no concept of a true vs. false variant; structurally Goodhart-prone.
The pattern across all of them
Generate creative, target, bid, optimize, orchestrate journeys — increasingly with autonomous agents and bandits. The capabilities we'd "invent" already exist at scale.
Gap: all optimize for engagement; none guarantee truth.

So what's actually missing? The same three holes, everywhere:

  • No claim verification. Generated copy is engagement-tuned and can hallucinate; every vendor tells you to review it. None bind a claim to a source.
  • No guardrail on the optimizer. Bandits chase CTR/conversion. Nothing stops them learning that an exaggerated claim "performs."
  • Wrong surface for trust. It's B2C ads and recommendations in walled gardens — not regulated, claims-heavy, named-prospect outbound where being wrong is a fine or a lawsuit.

3 · Our two differentiators

We match them on the commoditized parts — and win on the two that govern trust.

Differentiator 1
Provable generation

Every message is broken into atomic claims; each must bind to a real source and pass an independent entailment check, or it's rewritten or killed. A second gate blocks claims that are true but not legally sayable.

vs. big tech: they generate fast and tell you to review it. We generate and guarantee every claim is sourced — the layer none of them have.
Differentiator 2
Constrained self-optimization

Same contextual-bandit auto-A/B that Netflix and Meta use — but a variant only enters the arena after it passes verification. The optimizer can chase conversion all it wants; it can't discover a winning lie, because lies never compete.

vs. big tech: they optimize for engagement with no truth constraint. We take their proven technique and bound it to the truth — the safety guarantee they have no incentive to build.

The receipts — why each ✗ holds

Specific, current evidence behind the three gaps — so the matrix survives a skeptic.

✗ "Verify every claim to a source"

  • Google's own Super Bowl ad (Feb 2025). Gemini generated a cheesemonger's product copy claiming Gouda is "50–60% of global cheese consumption" — a confident fabrication. Google quietly edited the spot after backlash. If it ships a false claim in its own flagship ad, it isn't checking yours.
  • Salesforce's "Einstein Trust Layer" — the industry's flagship trust feature — does toxicity/bias filtering, data masking, and zero-retention privacy, and "reduces hallucination" via prompt instructions. None of that checks a claim against a source. The closest thing to "trust" in enterprise marketing AI still doesn't verify facts.
  • Every vendor's actual guidance is "disclose + review." Salesforce: label AI content with disclaimers/watermarks. Google's docs: "Gemini can hallucinate… plausible-sounding but factually incorrect." Disclosure and human review ≠ verification.

✗ "Optimizer bounded by truth"

  • The objective functions are engagement, full stop. Advantage+ optimizes ROAS; Performance Max optimizes conversions; contextual bandits (Netflix artwork) optimize plays/clicks. There is no truthfulness term in any of them — a claim that lifts CTR is, to the optimizer, a "better" claim, even if it's an exaggeration.
  • You can watch it happen. In the Gemini ad, the system produced the more compelling number, not the true one. Optimizing for "compelling" with no truth constraint is exactly how you drift to false-but-clicky.
  • The law assumes a constraint the tools don't have. The FTC's "Operation AI Comply" (2024–25) penalized unsubstantiated claims — Workado's "98% accurate" detector, IntelliVision's facial recognition. The optimizers have no concept of the substantiation the FTC requires.

✗ "Built for regulated, claims-heavy B2B"

  • For regulated verticals they restrict, not serve. Meta's 2025 "Sensitive / Financial Products & Services" categories strip health, wellness, and finance advertisers of lower-funnel conversion signals — a degraded algorithm, not a better one. The regulated advertiser gets less, not more.
  • Substantiation is entirely your problem. Pharma can't even name a prescription drug on Google or Meta without LegitScript / NABP certification; the platform polices what you may say and target — never whether a claim is true.
  • And the liability is yours. The FTC: "there is no AI exemption from the laws on the books." The Air Canada tribunal held the airline liable for its chatbot's false bereavement-fare claim. "The AI wrote it" is not a defense — the exact risk these tools leave uncovered.
  • It's not even their surface. B2B outbound to named prospects (cold email) is owned-channel, governed by CAN-SPAM / GDPR — not the ad platforms where this AI lives.
