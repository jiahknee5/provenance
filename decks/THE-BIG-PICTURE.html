Provenance is marketing you can prove — AI that writes a personal message to every prospect, and can't make a claim it can't back up.

The positioning, in one breath

For businesses whose claims carry risk — regulated, technical, or reputation-sensitive — Provenance is the AI marketing engine that personalizes outreach at scale while guaranteeing every claim is sourced and compliant. Unlike generic AI tools that write fast and hope, Provenance can't say anything it can't prove.

The bigger picture — why now

AI made marketing content infinite and free. That broke trust as fast as it broke cost.

  • The scarce thing is no longer copy — it's copy you can stand behind. Anyone can generate a thousand emails an hour. Almost no one can guarantee they're true.
  • The real gatekeepers are legal, compliance, and brand — not the creative team. They're where "AI at scale" quietly dies, because someone still has to read every word.
  • Regulators are moving. The FTC and sector watchdogs are actively policing AI-generated and unsubstantiated claims. "The AI wrote it" is not a defense.
  • So trust becomes the differentiator — and, done right, the moat. The brand that can prove its claims moves faster and sleeps better than the one that hopes.

The problem

AI marketing today is fast, fluent — and unaccountable.

What businesses face today
  • The AI makes things up. It will state "clinically proven" or "cuts costs 30%" with nothing behind it, in flawless prose.
  • One bad claim is real exposure. An FTC complaint, a lawsuit, a regulatory fine, a brand crisis — not a rounding error.
  • So humans review everything. The compliance queue is the bottleneck; personalization-at-scale dies waiting for sign-off.
  • No paper trail. When it goes wrong, you can't show what was claimed, or why it was allowed.
What Provenance changes
  • It can only say what it can prove. Verification is built into the writing, not bolted on after.
  • Every claim carries its receipt. Click any line, see the source that backs it.
  • Compliance scales with you. The rules run on every message automatically — no queue.
  • Full audit trail by default. Every claim, source, and decision is logged. Defensible to legal, regulators, and the board.

The solution — how it works

The AI writes a draft, then a verification engine puts every claim on trial before it can send: break it into individual claims → match each to a real source → confirm the source actually backs it (with independent checks, not one AI's opinion) → rewrite or kill anything unproven → block anything you're not legally allowed to say. Only a fully-backed message reaches a human for the final OK.

Draft: "At your 4,000 head, our product cuts feed 13% — $10/head in, $30 out — and it's the same enzyme as the egg version, allergen-free."

The artifact every business gets: a claim ledger — green (proven & cited), amber (rewritten to what's true), red (blocked). Proof you can show anyone.

And it improves itself — safely

The funnel A/B-tests itself across every segment — and can only ever discover winners that are true.

Once a message is verified, the engine keeps experimenting: it learns which angle, which proven claim to lead with, which channel, and which timing converts best for each kind of buyer — automatically shifting toward what works, the way Netflix learns which artwork you'll click. The difference is the guardrail: a variant only enters the test after it passes verification. So the system can chase conversion as hard as it likes and still never learn to win by exaggerating — because an unprovable claim never gets to compete.

Two halves of one idea.

The verifier makes self-optimization safe to turn on; the optimizer makes verified copy win. Most AI marketing optimizes for engagement and quietly drifts toward hype. This optimizes for engagement inside the boundary of what's true — the one thing big-tech ad AI structurally won't do.

Why it wins

Proof compounds.

Every campaign and every client adds verified claims and trusted sources to the engine — so it gets faster and safer over time, and a competitor starting from zero can't catch up. Trust is a moat you can't fake and can't buy. It's defensible in front of the people who actually decide: legal, regulators, and the board.
