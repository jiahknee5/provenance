→
Business use
Auto fact-checks and compliance-checks every line of every message before it sends.
→
Value

Claims Library — the system of record
Technical
A versioned claim store + source registry — every claim is a row bound to the document that proves it.
→
Business use
One approved source of truth: clear a claim once, reuse it everywhere; answer "where did that come from?" in a click.
→
Value
Cuts duplicated review cost, makes you audit-ready for regulators and the board, and compounds into a moat.① risk ↓  ② speed ↑
The Optimizer — truth-bounded auto-A/B
Technical
A contextual bandit per segment that tests only verified, cleared variants; reply/convert = reward, unsubscribe = penalty.
→
Business use
Continuously finds the best-performing message for each audience — no test setup, no waiting for significance.
→
Value
Higher conversion, continuously — with zero new liability. The upside of aggressive optimization without the downside.③ revenue ↑
Drift Monitor — keeps claims true over time
Technical
Watches sources for change/expiry; a claim-dependency graph triggers targeted re-verification.
→
Business use
Claims auto-update when the truth changes — a hold lifts, a stat updates, a reference customer churns.
→
Value
Prevents stale-claim liability, capitalizes the instant a claim clears, and removes manual re-audits.① risk ↓  ③ revenue ↑
Assurance Lab — proof the checker works
Technical
Generates adversarial traps (true claims mutated into subtle lies) and reports a reliability profile — catch-rate, false-reject, calibration, severity.
→
Business use
Gives Legal, the board, and prospects a continuous, numeric guarantee that the system actually catches bad claims.
→
Value
Turns "trust us" into an audited number — clears risk committees and accelerates regulated / enterprise sales.① risk ↓  → sales velocity

The three value levers, and what drives each

① Risk removed
→ ~0 unsubstantiated claims shipped

Every claim is provable and compliant, monitored for drift, and logged. The FTC's stance — "no AI exemption" — makes this existential in regulated categories.

Driven by: The Gate · Drift Monitor · Assurance Lab · Claims Library (audit)
② Speed & scale
→ review: weeks/asset → once/rule

Legal approves the library and the rules once; every message is checked instantly. Personalized variants go from "one safe generic version" to unlimited, all pre-cleared.

Driven by: The Gate (auto-clear) · Claims Library (clear once, reuse)
③ Revenue lifted
→ continuous conversion lift

Truth-bounded optimization keeps improving reply/convert per segment, and newly-cleared claims are capitalized the moment they're allowed — without adding risk.

Driven by: The Optimizer · Drift Monitor

What it's worth — a worked example

A regulated mid-market business running outbound (pharma, fintech, insurance, supplements).

Today
Legal/MLR reviews every asset — days to weeks per campaign.
Can't review 10,000 personalized variants → ships one generic version.
Baseline conversion; slow time-to-market.
Tail risk: one bad claim = six-/seven-figure fine or lawsuit.
With Provenance
Claim library + rules approved once; every variant cleared instantly.
1:1 personalization at scale — every variant pre-cleared.
Conversion lifts via truth-bounded optimization; ship same day.
Unsubstantiated-claim exposure → ~0, with a full audit trail.
Value = review-labor saved  +  revenue from personalization that was previously impossible  +  expected liability avoided  +  faster time-to-market. In regulated categories the risk + speed terms usually dominate; in growth-led ones, revenue does.
