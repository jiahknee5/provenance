This is the show. Presenter runs one HTML deck (←/→); the story builds on slides until the room’s phones become the prop. Below: beat-by-beat what the audience sees, hears, and feels — deck frames, live sites, facilitator moments, and Provenance panels woven into one narrative.

Act I · Frame the gap (§L) — presenter at laptop, cohort in seats, no phones yet
0:00L1Presenter opens cold: “Every tool in this room can put your name in a headline. None of them can prove the sentence is true.” Slide is one thesis line. Cohort nods — they’ve all seen creepy ad copy. Sets up: today you’ll feel personalization, then you’ll feel why it breaks.Deck
0:20L2Logos appear — Meta Advantage+, Google PMax, Salesforce Agentforce, Adobe Journey. Presenter names what each optimizes: reach, bids, journeys, content velocity. “They’re incredible at getting the right message to the right person.” No product pitch yet — just landscape everyone recognizes.Deck
0:40L3Split screen: left column “what they maximize” (CTR, ROAS, engagement); right column “what they don’t verify” (claim truth, regulatory fit, source freshness). Presenter: “The ad platform knows you clicked. It doesn’t know if ‘top 3% of cohort’ is true.”Deck
1:00L4Clay, Insider, Iterable, Braze, 6sense — the enrichment and journey layer. “They merge 50 attributes and generate copy in seconds.” Audience sees themselves: this is the stack they’d use to ship 1:1 at scale.Deck
1:20L5“Personalization AI” category slide — generate fast, legal/compliance reviews manually. Presenter lands the bottleneck: “Volume exploded. Review didn’t.” Someone in the room mutters about MLR backlogs.Deck
1:40L6Competitive matrix on screen — rows are vendors, columns are: verify claims? constrain optimizer? owned B2B channel? Most cells empty. Pause. “That’s the white space.”Deck
1:50L7Three holes called out visually: no system of record for claims, nothing stops the optimizer from rewarding a lie, no one detects when source data goes stale. Each hole gets one concrete Gauntlet-flavored example (program stat changes, guarantee language, Drew email stat outdated).Deck
2:00L8Close the frame: “We’re not rebuilding Meta. We’re adding the trust layer on email, web, and sales touchpoints you control.” Transition line: “Let me show you the funnel we’re about to run you through — literally.”Deck
Act II · Map the funnel (§F) — same persona path the live demo will use
2:10F1Classic five-stage funnel animates. Presenter traces a finger: “You’re somewhere on this curve. So is everyone next to you — but not the same place.” Seeds why two people get different copy later.Deck
2:25F2Diagram: paid social/search at top (Meta/Google own verification of delivery, not claims); owned middle/bottom (landing, email, web) where Provenance sits. “That’s where false claims become your liability.”Deck
2:40F3Touchpoint map lights up in order: X/IG ad → landing page → form → Drew email → night school attendance → retargeting → personalized web. Presenter: “Each step adds attributes. By the end the model knows a lot — the question is what it’s allowed to say.”Deck
2:55F4Claims Library icon on funnel — program facts, cohort stats, approved Drew excerpts, Gauntlet rules. “One place legal and marketing agree on what’s true.” Not built yet in their heads — named before they see it work.Deck
3:05F5Gate sits before render/send. Animation: copy flows in, sentences decompose, each claim gets green/amber/red. “Nothing ships red. Amber gets a disclaimer or human review.”Deck
3:15F6Optimizer branch — three CTA variants, metrics rising on the winner. A fourth variant labeled “guaranteed placement” is grayed out with a lock icon. “The bandit only picks from verified arms.”Deck
3:25F7Drift example: admissions rule changes from 3→5 years experience; old hero lines on 200 pages flip to stale badges. “One YAML edit, batch re-Gate — not 200 manual reviews.”Deck
Act III · Attribution story (§W · W1–W7) — “Meet Alex” before Alex becomes them
3:35W1Persona card: Alex — signed up via Gauntlet online form, director at a fintech, attended night school “Agents” session June 5, opened last Drew email. Feels like a CRM record. Presenter: “This is the Recipient Profile Graph. Your row looks like this.”Deck
4:00W2Mock X/IG ad on slide — hero image, emotional hook (“Stop shipping AI without guardrails”), UTM parameters visible in small type. “Alex clicks. We don’t know Alex yet — only the click.”Deck
4:20W3Landing page wireframe + attribution debugger panel beside it. Simulated events append: PageView, Lead — fake _fbp, GA4 client id, UTM campaign. Events are labeled SIMULATED. Room sees how pixels attach identity fragments, not full people.Deck
4:40W4Form fields animate into Alex’s profile: name, role, goals, night school interest. Presenter: “Submit stops anonymity — cookie set, CRM row created. We know who. We still haven’t verified what we’ll say to them.” Identity vs verification called out explicitly.Deck
5:00W5Drew email mock — subject line Alex would recognize. Open event fires on timeline: “opened in 90 seconds.” Profile gains a warm behavioral signal. Someone laughs: “That’s literally me with Drew’s emails.”Deck
5:15W6Night school attendance row drops in — session date, topic. Copy preview updates on slide: “Great seeing you at Agents night…” Presenter: “Real Gauntlet data. This is what Track A and Track B will both use.”Deck
5:30W7Batch enrichment output — Meta interest categories, LinkedIn seniority, a few T4 social signals with orange SIMULATED badges. “Ran 48 hours ago, not live on your phone right now.” Sets expectation: some signals on the live site are representative, not scraped live.Deck
Act IV · The gut punch (§W live · Track A) — phones come out, energy shifts
5:45W8QR code fills the slide. Presenter: “Scan it. This is your page, not Alex’s.” Rustle of phones — cohort opens magic links. Track A loads: personalized hero with their real name, night school reference, signup source; sidebar “data we used” may show Meta interests or “opened Drew email quickly”; SANDBOX watermark visible. Audible reactions — “whoa,” “that’s my session.” Load target <2s on venue WiFi.Live · Track A
6:30—Presenter lets the room breathe. People turn screens to neighbors: different headlines, different CTAs, different “proof” blocks. “Why does yours mention fintech and mine mentions health?” Micro-segmentation becomes visceral — not a diagram, a pocket experience. Presenter asks: “How did it know that?” — lets answers spill (form data, email, attendance, simulated signals).Live · room
7:00—Facilitator (second laptop) projects Edge-A — planted profile “Jordan.” Track A hero implies guaranteed capstone placement / income outcome. Presenter reads the line aloud. Room gets quiet. “Could Gauntlet send this email?” Head shakes. FTC / educational advertising mentioned. Same data richness, wrong claim — nobody wants this at scale.Edge-A
7:30—Edge-B on screen — health-adjacent language tied to a fabricated personal detail (e.g. dietary inference from signup). Reactions shift from “illegal” to “creepy.” Presenter: “Track A didn’t invent your allergy — we planted this on a sandbox profile to show where enrichment + LLM drift.” Badge on simulated fields where applicable.Edge-B
8:00—Edge-C — “#1 rated cohort experience,” false urgency countdown, competitor shade without source. Feels like normal growth marketing — that’s the point. “You’ve all written something this aggressive. Now imagine 200 variants by Friday with no review.”Edge-C
8:30—Presenter steps back from slides, addresses room: “That felt amazing. Why can’t we ship Track A to the whole cohort tomorrow?” Brief discussion — legal, brand, manual review, “one bad claim.” Land: true ≠ allowed; verification doesn’t scale by hand. Bridge: “Same person. Same data. Watch what Provenance does.”Discuss
Act V · The fix (§W live + panels) — same token, different world
9:00W9Presenter: “Pull up the same link — toggle to governed.” Cohort flips to Track B (or facilitator demos on projector with a known token). Hero still personal — name, session, role — but copy is tighter; exaggerated stats gone. Tap “Inspect claims”: ledger overlays hero — green with source links, amber with disclaimers. Audience clicks a green claim, sees Drew email stat or program doc excerpt. Zero red in hero.Live · Track B
9:45H4On projector, facilitator walks Edge-A’s page on Track B: the guarantee line appears struck-through in inspect mode; ledger shows RED with rule_id “no-income-guarantee.” Presenter reads the rule. “Track A would have sent it. Track B structurally cannot render it in the hero.” Same RPG, different pipeline outcome.Live · Gate
10:15H8Assurance Lab panel on screen — table of 20+ adversarial traps (false stats, subtle guarantees, hallucinated cohort rankings). Metrics: catch-rate ~90%+, false-reject <5%. Side-by-side with “LLM judge only” baseline underperforming. “This audits the Gate — not your marketing team at 2am.”Assurance
10:45H6Optimizer panel: leaderboard for Alex’s micro-segment — three CTA variants, reply rate climbing on the winner over simulated weeks. Fourth row “Guaranteed placement” grayed, locked out of consideration. Presenter: “The model wants to pick it — structure prevents it. You can’t win by lying.”Optimizer
11:15H7Drift demo live: facilitator opens tenant YAML on laptop, changes one admissions rule (3→5 years experience), runs re-Gate on Alex’s profile. Within 60s, Alex’s page shows a stale badge on the old claim, then refreshed ledger after re-run. Room sees policy change propagate without rewriting copy by hand.Drift
Act VI · Close (§P · W10) — name the primitive, invite the build
11:45P4Full stack diagram returns — Library → Gate → Optimizer → Render → Drift, Assurance auditing Gate. Presenter ties each box to something the room just saw (ledger, grayed variant, stale badge, traps table). “This isn’t a feature list. You watched it.”Deck
12:00P2–3Two differentiators on screen: provable generation (every sentence has provenance) and constrained self-optimization (performance without reward-hacking). Quick, no jargon — callbacks to phone moment and grayed CTA.Deck
12:15P6Gauntlet pilot slide — Prime/Catalyst/hiring partner routing as second tenant story. Optional: show GAUNTLET-PILOT-DECK QR for people who want the GTM thread. “You’re the pilot customer — you felt the problem first.”Deck
12:30W10Single line on screen: “You felt the problem on your phone. This is the fix.” Presenter quiet for a beat. Cohort puts phones down — energy reflective, not sold-to.Deck
12:45—Ask: join the capstone build, Jun 29 showcase, follow the repo. Q&A — likely questions on latency, NLI vs rules, real Meta integration. Facilitator keeps Edge profiles and staging URL ready for side demos.Live

Approach

One web app, two tracks — /w/{token} with ?track=ungoverned|provenance, not two codebases. Batch everything 48h before show; no live LLM on stage. Pre-provision identity from Tom’s export via magic link/QR; optional form sets cookie for return visits. Simulate pixels and Tier 3–4 data with visible badges.
