A claim is any specific assertion that can be wrong, impermissible, or stale — in an email, on a webpage, in an agent reply, in a filing, in a tutor's explanation. Provenance tracks each claim through its life: draft → verify against source → clear against rules → (optionally optimize among verified variants) → ship → monitor for drift → prove the checker works.

Where else this is useful

🤖
AI agents & copilots

Any agent that speaks or acts on behalf of an org generates claims faster than humans can check them — support refunds, HR policy answers, tool-use justifications, custom proposals.

Ship becomes reply · execute · display. Same Gate; "send" is just one adapter.
⚖️
Regulated professional content

Pharma MLR, financial advisor communications, insurance policy summaries, legal memos — industries that already have human review queues at every variant.

Replace per-document review with per-rule governance. Legal sets rules once.
📊
Compliance, risk & reporting

ESG disclosures, regulatory filings, audit narratives, citizen-facing eligibility explanations — specific numbers and statements that must trace to source data.

Drift Monitor is the hero here. Stale claims are worse than wrong marketing copy.
🎓
Education & training

AI tutors, role-based compliance training, certification prep — personalized explanations that must match canonical curriculum and policy.

Claims library = approved curriculum. Gate = "is this entailed by the source material?"
📰
Research & enterprise knowledge

RAG chatbots, research assistants, internal wikis — the failure mode is confident wrong answers with no citation.

Provenance is RAG with a mandatory citation gate and staleness tracking.
💻
Software & technical docs

AI-generated docs, API assistants, incident comms, security questionnaires — assertions must match specs, telemetry, and control matrices.

API doc changes → dependency graph invalidates every dependent generated snippet.

Why marketing is still the right capstone demo

Not because the technology is marketing-only — because marketing makes every hard property visible at once:

High-volume personalization · verification bottleneck · true ≠ allowed · stale sources · reward-hacking · audit trail. A compliance officer or platform engineer watches the showcase and maps it to their own queue: that's our support bot, our MLR, our filing draft.

The capstone builds on a standalone demo tenant (email MVP + website adapter in the showcase). Second industry = config, not a rebuild — same Claims Library, same Gate, different sources and rules.

Personalization without proof is a lawsuit.
Governed generation with proof is infrastructure.

Marketing is the loudest room to prove it. The application is anywhere a claim meets scale.
