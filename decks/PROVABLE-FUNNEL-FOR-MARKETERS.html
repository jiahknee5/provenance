The Provable Funnel

AI outreach that can't lie

⟶ plain-English version (no jargon)

It writes a personal message to every prospect — and it physically can't make a claim you can't back up. Every sentence either shows its receipts, or it doesn't go out.

"Personalize at scale" and "don't get us sued" usually fight each other. This makes them the same thing.

AI can already write a thousand tailored emails an hour. The problem is you can't trust it near customers — it makes things up, exaggerates, and one bad claim is an FTC complaint, a lawsuit, or a blown-up brand. So today a human has to read everything, and that is the bottleneck. We move the fact-check inside the writing — so trust stops being a manual step.

Think of it as

Spell-check, but for truth and compliance. Like having your compliance reviewer read every message before it sends — instantly, on every line, never tired, never asleep.

The problem, in marketing terms

  • AI writes fast and sounds confident — even when it's wrong. It will happily claim "clinically proven" or "cuts costs 30%" with nothing behind it.
  • In regulated industries, a single bad claim is real money. Pharma, finance, insurance, food, supplements, health — the wrong sentence is a fine or a lawsuit, not a typo.
  • So legal/compliance reviews everything by hand. That review queue is where personalization-at-scale goes to die. You either send generic safe copy, or you wait weeks for sign-off.
  • Nobody can prove what the AI actually said and why. When it goes wrong, there's no paper trail — just "the AI wrote it."

What it does — in four plain steps

It writes the personal message.

Pulls real facts about that specific prospect (their size, their products, their situation) and drafts tailored outreach — same as a good AI tool today.

It breaks the message into individual claims.

Every promise or statement is pulled out separately — "4,000 head," "cuts feed 13%," "allergen-free" — so each can be checked on its own.

It demands a receipt for each claim.

Each claim has to match a real source document. If the source genuinely backs it up, it gets a green light and a citation. If not, it's flagged. (And it doesn't trust just one AI's opinion — several independent checks have to agree, because one can be wrong.)

It fixes or kills anything that can't be proven.

Unsupported lines get rewritten to what's actually true, or removed. And things that are true but you're not legally allowed to say yet (like unpublished trial data) get blocked too. Only a fully-backed message goes to a human for the final OK.

If the engineers say a word you don't know

Claim
Any statement or promise in your copy — "cuts costs 30%," "clinically proven," "4,000 head."
Hallucination
When the AI confidently states a "fact" that simply isn't true. The thing we're stopping.
Source / citation / receipt
The real document or data that proves a claim. Every surviving line has one.
Verification gate
The built-in fact-checker that approves, rewrites, or blocks each claim before send.
"Cited or killed"
Our rule: a claim either gets a receipt, or it gets removed. No exceptions.
"True but not sayable"
A claim that's actually true but you're legally not allowed to say yet (e.g., unpublished data). Blocked anyway.
